Methodology

How I think about AI adoption: a prompting framework, a rubric for picking workflows, and a map for prioritizing investments.

The 5 P’s of Prompting

A framework I developed for corporate AI workshops. For non-technical teams to consistently get useful output from AI tools:

  • Premise — what’s the situation? Audience, goal, constraints.
  • Persona — what role should the AI take?
  • Pattern — what does good look like? Show one or two examples.
  • Purpose — what specifically should it produce?
  • Presentation — what format and length?

Used as the spine of the corporate AI workshops.

The AI Sweet Spot

A rubric for deciding whether a task is worth automating with AI. Four properties:

  • High repetition — you do this often (weekly or daily).
  • Language-heavy — primarily reading, writing, or transforming text.
  • Low-to-medium stakes — mistakes are recoverable.
  • Privacy-safe — no PII, regulated data, confidential strategy.

When all four apply, AI gives outsized leverage. When two or fewer apply, AI is probably more friction than help.
